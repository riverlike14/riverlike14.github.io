# Philosophy of Technical Analysis

## Introduction

- Define technical analysis
- Discuss the philosophical premises
- Distinguish between technical and fundamental analysis
- Address criticisms raised against the technical analysis

> *Technical analysis is the study of market action, primarily through the use of charts, for the purpose of forecasting future price trends.*

market action(price action) - price, volume, open interest(선물 옵션 등에서 미청산 계약)

## Philosophy or Rationale

1. *Market action discounts everything.*
2. *Prices move in trends.*
3. *History repeats itself.*

### Market Action Discounts Everything

- Anything that can affect the price is reflected in the price of that market.
  - The study of that market price is all that is necessary.
- Price action should reflect shifts in supply and demand.
  - If prices are rising demand must exceed supply and the fundamentals must be bullish, and vice versa.
  - Knowing the reasons why prices rise or fall is not necessary in the forecasting process.

### Prices Move in Trends

- The concept of ***trend*** is absolutely essential to the technical approach.
  - Identify trends and follow the direction of those trends.
  - *"A trend in motion is more likely to continue than to reverse."*

### History Repeats Itself

- Chart patterns are based on the study of *human psychology*.
  - Human psychology does not tend to change.
  - They will work in the future as well.

## Technical versus Fundamental Forecasting

> *The fundamentalist studies the cause of market movement, while the technician studies the effect*

- The fundamental approach examines all of the relevant factors affecting the market price
- Prices, while the known fundamentals have already been discounted, are reacting to the unknown fundamentals.
  - Market price tends to lead the known fundamentals.
  - Charts and fundamentals are often in conflict with each other.

## Analysis versus Timing

- Timing, determining entry and exit points, is technical.
